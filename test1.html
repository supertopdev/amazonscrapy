Using as ramp for disabled cat
By Kristina Dee on December 18, 2013
Verified Purchase
I am using this as a ramp for a disabled cat to get onto my bed. It was the longest I could find; I had tried a 24 inch sisal scratching wall post, but that was too short, as the incline was too steep, even though my bed is only 18" high. At over 3 feet, this is very long, which makes the incline gentle for her, and also is very wide. (She had a reaction to a blood transfusion as a kitten which left her neurologically impaired, but saved her life). The ramp also is quite soft. My fully abled cats enjoy walking up and down the "ramp," as well. I have another solid cat house (Bootsie's bunk bed) at the foot of it so it won't slide, and also tied it to the bed using twine to further secure it. I have it placed along the length of my bed, parallel to it, so my bed provides a bit of a railing which she can lean against if she wanted to help her balance. I am very happy with the length and width of this.

Mine was a charcoal color with a pattern on the carpet, close to the photo. I was actually hoping it would be green, as other reviewers had received, to match the decor of my room, but the charcoal color is very neutral and blends in anyway. The top and bottom have little pawprints on the rubberized sections - cute little detail.
